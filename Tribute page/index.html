William Dampier

The first person to circumnavigate the globe three times and write a book 'bout it.

Here's a timeline of William Dampier life:

  • 1651 - Born in East Coker, Somerset, England. He was baptised on 5th of September, but his precise date of birth is not recorded.
  • 1673 - Joined the Royal Navy, but his service was cut short by a catastrophic illness.
  • 1679 - Dampier joined the crew of the buccaneer Captain Bartholomew Sharp in Central America.
  • 1691 - Dampier returned to England from his first circumnavigation penniless, with his only possessions being his journals and a tattooed slave.
  • 1697 - The publication of the book, "A New Voyage Round the World".
  • 1699 - Dampier was given command of "HMS Roebuck", that carried the first Brittish scintific expedition to Australia. His account of the expedition was later published as "Voyage to New Holland".
  • 1703 - Second circumnavigation as commander of 26-gun ship "St George".
  • 1708 - Third circumnavigation on the privateer "Duke" as a pilot.
  • 1715 - Dampier died at the age of 64 in London. The exact date and circumstances of his death, also his final resting place, are all unknown.

"Seventeenth-century pirate genius William Dampier sailed around the world three times when crossing the Pacific was a major feat, was the first explorer to visit all five continents, and reached Australia eighty years before Captain Cook. Swift and Defoe used his experiences in writing Gulliver's Travels and Robinson Crusoe. Dampier's description of breadfruit was the impetus for Captain Bligh's voyage on the Bounty. He was so influential that today he has more than one thousand entries in the Oxford English Dictionary, including such words as chopsticks, barbecue, and kumquat. "

-- from his biography: "A Pirate of Exquisite Mind"
